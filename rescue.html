Aurora Rescue Network

Help us locate and protect children in unsafe situations

See Something? Say Something.

If you notice a child living on the streets, forced into begging, or in any unsafe environment, you can help. The Aurora Rescue Network allows community members to report children in need so our outreach team can respond quickly and provide safety, shelter, and care.

Please share as much detail as possible. Every piece of information helps us locate the child and understand the urgency of the situation.

🕒

Our outreach team monitors reports round the clock.

How Our Rescue Process Works

A coordinated response to bring children into safety

📩

1. Submit a Report

Share details about the child, location, and situation. Include photos or videos if possible and safe.

👂

2. Verification Call

Our caseworkers review the report and contact you for any additional information or clarification.

🚑

3. Outreach Visit

A trained rescue team visits the location, providing immediate support and coordinating with authorities if needed.

🏠

4. Safe Intake

The child is transported to Aurora House or a partner safe shelter for medical evaluation, food, and care.

📝

5. Documentation

We document the child’s background, needs, and preferences to plan the best care pathway.

🌱

6. Ongoing Support

The child receives education, counseling, healthcare, and family integration support.

Information We Need

Provide as much detail as you safely can

📍

Location Details

  • Exact address or landmark
  • Common times the child is present
  • Nearby shops, shelters, or transport hubs
🕒

Timeline & Frequency

  • When you first noticed the child
  • How many days/weeks they’ve been there
  • How often you see them (daily, weekly, etc.)
👕

Child’s Description

  • Approximate age and gender
  • Clothing and physical description
  • Visible injuries or health concerns
⚠️

Safety Concerns

  • Is anyone accompanying or controlling the child?
  • Any signs of abuse or trafficking?
  • Immediate dangers (traffic, weather, etc.)
🎥

Evidence

  • Photos or videos (if safe to collect)
  • Names/contact of other witnesses
  • Police or authority reference numbers
📞

Your Contact Info

  • Best way to reach you quickly
  • Times we can call
  • Are you willing to accompany our team?

Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding the rescue and intake process

Can I submit a report anonymously?

+

You can request anonymity when we contact you, but providing contact details helps us verify information quickly. We keep all reporter information strictly confidential.

What if the child moves frequently?

+

Share all locations or routes you have seen the child. Our team will monitor the area and coordinate with local partners to locate the child.

What happens after the child is rescued?

+

The child is brought to a safe location where they receive medical care, counseling, and basic needs. We assess their background and work with authorities to determine the best long-term plan.

Will I be notified about the child’s status?

+

Yes, we provide updates to the reporting individual when possible, respecting privacy laws and safety guidelines.

Can I volunteer to support rescues?

+

Absolutely. Indicate your interest in the form or contact us via the volunteer section. We provide training for volunteer outreach roles.
